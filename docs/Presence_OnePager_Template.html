[APP NAME] — The easiest way to meet up with friends nearby

[YOUR TAGLINE HERE] — e.g., “Know when friends are around—without group‑chat chaos or creepy live tracking.”

Problem

Spontaneous meetups die in endless chats: “who’s around?”, “who’s down?”, “how far are you?” Location sharing is either too vague or always‑on and invasive.

Solution

A lightweight, privacy‑first app that shows:

  • How many friends are at a place now
  • Who’s interested in going
  • Travel times (drive/walk) for interested friends
  • Optional map view for selected friends
  • A home screen widget for quick glance

Everything is explicit and time‑boxed (e.g., “share for 30 minutes”). No always‑on tracking.

Core features

  • Share a plan in two taps — place + time window; choose who can see it.
  • See where the action is — “X here • Y interested • ETAs”.
  • One‑tap Interested — friends see your travel time automatically.
  • Privacy controls — precise or approximate; sharing auto‑stops; block/ghost modes.
  • Battery‑friendly — low‑power location + geofences; no constant GPS.
  • Home screen widget — top nearby spots at a glance.

Why it’s different

  • Time‑boxed presence, not tracking: you choose when to share; it ends automatically.
  • Decisions at a glance: counts & ETAs, not a stalker map.
  • Frictionless: two taps to share; one tap to join.
  • Privacy by default: approximate sharing, minimal data retention, clear controls.

How it works (simple)

  1. Start a plan (“Coffee at Blue Bottle, 3–4pm”) or tap “Share now”.
  2. Friends see a card: 3 here • 5 interested • ETAs.
  3. They tap Interested; travel times show up.
  4. Meet up—then sharing auto‑stops.

Who it’s for

  • Urban 20–35 year‑olds who already meet casually after work / weekends.
  • University groups and hobby communities (volleyball, climbing, gaming).
  • Co‑located remote teammates who want spontaneous lunches or co‑working.

Safety & privacy

  • Explicit consent every time; sharing is off by default.
  • Approximate location option (venue‑level or coarse area).
  • Short data life: presence auto‑expires; long‑term we keep anonymous counts only.
  • Block/report from day one.

Early proof (Private Preview goals)

  • 50–150 invited testers in two cities.
  • D1 retention ≥ 50%; 2+ meetups/week across the cohort.
  • Crash‑free ≥ 99.5%; battery impact ≤ 3%/day for casual use.

Business model (phased)

  • Start free — build trust and habit.
  • Premium — advanced circles, smarter notifications, power‑user controls.
  • Optional, privacy‑safe — anonymous foot‑traffic insights and opt‑in promos. Never sell personal location.

Why now

  • Friends are meeting in person again; group chats don’t solve “who’s around right now?”.
  • Users want intent and signal, not another social feed.
  • Modern mobile tech makes this smooth and battery‑friendly.

Roadmap highlights (next)

  • Invite system & simple friend graph.
  • “Ping me when ≥2 friends are here” notifications.
  • Smarter ETA caching for reliability and low cost.
  • First‑run tutorial and clear policy disclosures.

Sharing is explicit and time‑boxed, and there’s a home‑screen widget for quick glances. It’s the fastest way to decide if it’s worth heading over—built privacy‑first.”
